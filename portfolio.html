Lab 3 – Census Mapping and Visualization

Total Population Choropleth

This choropleth map visualizes total population by census tract in King County, Washington using 2020 Decennial Census data. Darker colors represent census tracts with larger populations, while lighter areas contain fewer residents. The map highlights how population is concentrated in the western urban corridor surrounding Seattle while eastern portions of the county are more rural and sparsely populated.

Dot Density Map

This dot density map represents population distribution across King County, Washington. Each dot represents a fixed number of residents, helping visualize population clustering and settlement patterns. Dense clusters appear around Seattle and nearby suburbs, while eastern portions of the county show significantly lower population density.

Young Persons Graduated Symbols

This graduated symbol map displays the population under age 18 by census tract in King County, Washington using ACS 2020 estimates. Larger symbols represent tracts with greater numbers of young residents. The map demonstrates how family-oriented suburban areas tend to contain larger youth populations than central urban neighborhoods.

Lab 5 – Spatial Analysis and Modeling

Portland-Vancouver Metro Tracts

This map displays census tracts across the Portland-Vancouver- Hillsboro metropolitan area spanning Oregon and Washington. The metropolitan boundary is shown using a bold outline, illustrating how metropolitan regions frequently cross state boundaries. The map highlights the geographic scale and spatial organization of the metro area.

Income Hotspot Analysis

This Getis-Ord Gi* hotspot analysis identifies statistically significant clusters of high and low median household income within the Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue metropolitan area. Red areas indicate significant high-income clusters while blue areas represent low-income clusters. The map demonstrates how spatial clustering can reveal patterns of inequality across metropolitan regions.

Lab 7 – Segregation and Diversity

Seattle MSA Segregation Map

This map visualizes multigroup segregation across the Seattle metropolitan area using ACS demographic data. The analysis incorporates White, Black, Asian, and Hispanic populations to measure segregation across census tracts. The map demonstrates how racial and ethnic groups are distributed unevenly throughout the metropolitan region.

Asian Location Quotient

This location quotient map measures the relative concentration of Asian residents across census tracts in King County, Washington. Areas with higher values indicate neighborhoods where the Asian population is more concentrated relative to the county average. The map highlights spatial clustering patterns and demographic concentration throughout the region.

Lab 9 – Spatial Modeling

GWR Income Map

This geographically weighted regression (GWR) map visualizes spatial variation in modeled relationships across the Seattle metropolitan area. GWR allows regression coefficients to vary across geographic space, revealing localized differences in how variables interact throughout the study region.

K-Means Cluster Map

This k-means cluster analysis groups census tracts into clusters based on similarities in demographic and spatial variables. Different colors represent unique clusters, allowing patterns and geographic groupings to emerge across the Seattle metropolitan area. The analysis demonstrates how unsupervised machine learning can identify spatial similarities within urban regions.
